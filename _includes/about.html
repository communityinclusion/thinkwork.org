Got 44 Minutes to Think About Employment?

Does All Mean All? Culturally Diverse Families and Access to Services Wednesday December 9, 3pm - 3:44pm Eastern

Join us for the next webinar in our "44" series! We'll be looking at how culturally diverse families access employment services for their family members with disabilities.

Researchers Judith Gross (University of Kansas) and Grace Francis (George Mason University) have worked intensively with Hispanic families in rural areas of Kansas. They will talk about the importance of engaging culturally and linguistically diverse families in services for their children with disabilities. They'll also discuss the barriers these families face, and offer strategies for professionals to help ensure full access to services.

We'll also hear from family members and individuals with disabilities about how culture and language impact their use of employment services.

This webinar is free and open to everyone.

About

Welcome. This project is home to research, training, and outreach activities that promote employment for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD).

We are funded by the National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research in the US Department of Education.

The RRTC is a project of the Institute for Community Inclusion at the University of Massachusetts Boston.
